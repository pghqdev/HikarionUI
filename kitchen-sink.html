Elevation

Five rungs, --elevation-0 through --elevation-4, each meaning a material state rather than a size. A crisp contact shadow plus one ambient layer, inked from the theme’s accent so a theme swap re-lights the whole ladder. --shadow-sm, --shadow and --shadow-lg alias rungs 1, 2 and 4.

0 — flush

In the page. The hairline separates.

1 — raised

Attached: cards, fields, buttons.

2 — lifted

Picked up under the pointer.

3 — floating

Off the page: menus, tooltips.

4 — overlay

Above everything: dialogs, toasts.

Under data-density="compact" the ladder is unchanged — depth is not a spacing concern; only the card chrome tightens.

What is Hikarion?

Bare <details>/<summary>. The chevron rotates on --ease-hikarion; the toggle is native, no JS.

Does it need JavaScript?

No — disclosure is native. hikarion.js is only for the few things CSS can't do.

What about reduced motion?

The chevron transition is disabled by the global prefers-reduced-motion killswitch.

Skeleton

[data-skeleton] — a pure-CSS loading placeholder. The element’s own text sets the bar’s shape, so there is no sizing API. Reduced motion flattens the sweep. Bars don’t shrink under Compact — they stand in for body copy, which doesn’t either; the card padding around them is what tightens.
